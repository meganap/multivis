Definitions

  • A taxon is group or category of living organisms linked by common ancestry.
  • Increased diversity is a larger difference in both number and abundance of present organisms within a sample.
  • Two groups are similar if they are similar in both abundance and type of present organisms.

Definitions

  • A phylogeny is an inferred evolutionary relationship between organisms.
  • A phylogenetic tree is a visual representation of the inferred evolutionary relationships between organisms.
  • Closely related in terms of phylogeny means that organisms are closer to each other in evolutionary time.
  • Branch length is a representation of evolutionary time. A longer branch length means that the organism has not changed for a longer evolutionary time.
  • Depth refers to the number of ancestors that a particular organism has. An organism with many ancestors has many changes throughout evolutionary time.

Definitions

  • A principal coordinate is a numerical representation that describes the similarity of points. It is often mapped to an axis in a visual representation. The first principle coordinate describes the most difference between points.
  • Spread in principal coordinate space refers to the closeness of points. If the points have more spread, the farther they are away from each other in space.
  • If two points are closer to each other in space then their communities are closely related/similar.
